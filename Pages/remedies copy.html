Remedy for Mild Cough

▼

Details & Benefits:

A traditional mixture to soothe throat irritation and ease mild coughs, using common kitchen ingredients.

Steps:

  • Mix 1 teaspoon of honey with 1/4 teaspoon of fresh ginger juice.
  • Add a pinch of black pepper powder.
  • Consume this mixture slowly, 2-3 times a day.
  • Avoid cold water immediately after consumption.

Remedy for Indigestion

▼

Details & Benefits:

This simple decoction (Kashayam) aids digestion and can help relieve bloating or discomfort after meals.

Steps:

  • Boil 1 cup of water.
  • Add 1/2 teaspoon of cumin seeds (Jeeragam) and 1/4 teaspoon of coriander seeds (Dhaniya).
  • Optionally, add a small piece of crushed dry ginger (Sukku).
  • Simmer for 5 minutes, then strain.
  • Drink warm after meals.

Skin Soother for Minor Rashes

▼

Details & Benefits:

A cooling paste using sandalwood, known in Siddha for its soothing properties on irritated skin (test on a small patch first).

Steps:

  • Take a small amount of pure sandalwood powder (Santhanam).
  • Mix with a few drops of rose water or plain cool water to form a smooth paste.
  • Apply a thin layer to the affected area (avoid broken skin).
  • Let it dry naturally, then gently rinse off.
  • Discontinue if irritation occurs.

Headache Relief Balm (External)

▼

Details & Benefits:

A traditional topical application thought to ease tension headaches.

Steps:

  • Crush a small piece of dry ginger (Sukku).
  • Mix the powder with a few drops of water to make a thick paste.
  • Apply a small amount gently on the forehead (avoiding eyes).
  • Rest for 15-20 minutes. May cause a warm sensation.
  • Wash off gently. Use cautiously.
